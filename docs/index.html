Grid Frame

The grid frame is the base structure for creating a full application experience. .grid-frame will use the full height of browser and setup flexbox. 

<body>
  <div class="grid-frame">
    <div class="grid-block"></div>
    <div class="grid-block"></div>
  </div>
</body>

Grid Block

.grid-block is the foundation of the grid system. It can create columns and rows. They can also have a defined size and reorderd. 

<div class="grid-block">
  <div class="grid-block"></div>
  <div class="grid-block"></div>
</div>

By default, the code above will create columns:

If you want to change the layout to be vertical instead, checkout out the vertical grid section.

Grid Content

.grid-content behaves like .grid-block But .grid-content will have left and right padding by default. And, as the name implies, it is meant to display content.

Given the nature of flexbox, if you use any element within .grid-block without the class grid-content you can see bizarre layout. For that reason it is recommended to use .grid-content for content.

.grid-content
.grid-content
<div class="grid-block">
  <div class="grid-content">.grid-content</div>
  <div class="grid-content">.grid-content</div>
</div>

Grid Content without padding

To remove the default padding of .grid-content add the class collapse. 

<div class="grid-block">
  <div class="grid-content collapse">.grid-content without padding</div>
  <div class="grid-content collapse">.grid-content without padding</div>
</div>
.grid-content without padding
.grid-content without padding

Intermixing Grid Block and Grid Content

Both classes can be intermixed to achieve your desired layout.

.grid-content
.grid-block
<div class="grid-block">
  <div class="grid-content">.grid-content</div>
  <div class="grid-block">.grid-block</div>
</div>

Column Expanding

If you don't define a column width, it will expand to fill the remaining container width. 

<div class="grid-block">
  <div class="grid-block small-3"></div>
  <div class="grid-block"></div>
</div>
3

Column Collapsing

If you want to shrink the width of a container to match the content add the class of shrink. 

<div class="grid-block">
  <div class="grid-block shrink">Some Content</div>
  <div class="grid-block"></div>
</div>

Vertical Grid

This is the cool part.

Vertical grids are defined be adding a vertical class to the parent .grid-block

It's difficult to see what is happening here. It just looks like a couple of rows doesn't it. However, each .grid-block under .vertical will stretch to fill the space.

Here is a better example, showing vertical grid next to a single .grid-block. 

<div class="grid-block">
  <div class="grid-block vertical">
    <div class="grid-block"></div>
    <div class="grid-block"></div>
  </div>
  <div class="grid-block">I am the same height as the two rows</div>
</div>
I am the same height as the two rows

Vertical Column Collapsing

Like columns, the vertical columns can also be collapsed by adding the class of shrink. 

<div class="grid-block">
  <div class="grid-block vertical">
    <div class="grid-block">I expand since the other content shrinks</div>
    <div class="grid-block shrink">Box shrinks to fit content</div>
  </div>
  <div class="grid-block vertical">
    <div class="grid-block shrink">Box shrinks to fit content</div>
    <div class="grid-block">I expand since the other content shrinks</div>
  </div>
</div>

Intermixing vertical and horizontal classes

To accomodate desktop, mobile and everything in between, flexbox-for-apps allows you to define different layouts (vertical vs. horizontal) on different screen sizes.

.small-vertical, .medium-vertical and .large-vertical cause their children to have vertical layouts at their respective breakpoints.

I will be on top starting at the small breakpoint.
I will be on top starting at the small breakpoint.
I will be on top starting at the large breakpoint.
I will be on bottom starting at the large breakpoint. 
<div class="grid-block">
  <div class="grid-block small-vertical">
    <div class="grid-content">
      I will be on top starting at the small breakpoint.
    </div>
    <div class="grid-content">
      I will be on top starting at the small breakpoint.
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="grid-block large-vertical">
    <div class="grid-content">
      I will be on top starting at the large breakpoint.
    </div>
    <div class="grid-content">
      I will be on bottom starting at the large breakpoint.
    </div>
  </div>
</div>

The same goes for horizontal layouts. .small-horizontal, .medium-horizontal and .large-horizontal cause their children to have horizontal layouts at their respective breakpoints.

I will be on top at the small breakpoint and horizontal starting at medium.
I will be on bottom at the small breakpoint and horizontal starting at medium.
I will be on top until at the large breakpoint.
I will be on bottom until at the large breakpoint. 
<div class="grid-block">
  <div class="grid-block small-vertical medium-horizontal">
    <div class="grid-content">
      I will be on top at the small breakpoint and horizontal starting at medium.
    </div>
    <div class="grid-content">
      I will be on bottom at the small breakpoint and horizontal starting at medium.
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="grid-block vertical large-horizontal">
    <div class="grid-content">
      I will be on top until at the large breakpoint.
    </div>
    <div class="grid-content">
      I will be on bottom until at the large breakpoint.
    </div>
  </div>
</div>
